CHARLES BUKOWSKI

"Find what you love and let it kill you."

Writer • Poet • Legend (1920-1994)

About Bukowski

Charles Bukowski was a German-American poet, novelist, and short story writer. His writing was influenced by the social, cultural, and economic ambience of his home city of Los Angeles.

Early Life

Born in Andernach, Germany in 1920, Bukowski moved to Los Angeles at age 3. His early experiences with poverty and his father's abuse would later influence his raw, honest writing style.

Writing Career

Starting with poetry in small literary magazines, Bukowski went on to write over 40 books of poetry and prose, including novels like "Post Office" and "Ham on Rye".

Legacy

Known for his uncompromising voice and underground sensibility, Bukowski remains one of the most widely read American writers, translated into dozens of languages.
